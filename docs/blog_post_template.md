Blog Post Draft
===============

Title
-----
How I built an agentic Nova system to prevent rural vaccine cold chain failures

Introduction
------------
I built this project because vaccine delivery risk is often highest where infrastructure is weakest. In many regions, clinics depend on fragile refrigeration systems, and failures are detected after doses are already compromised.

What I built
------------
I created Nova Cold Chain Guardian, a multi-agent AI workflow that unifies sensor telemetry, visual inspection evidence, and technician voice transcripts. Instead of a single generic model call, I orchestrate specialized agents that produce auditable evidence and concrete actions.

Why this matters for communities
--------------------------------
This approach can reduce avoidable vaccine waste, improve continuity of immunization programs, and strengthen trust in public health delivery. The direct community benefit is better access to potent vaccines and lower risk of missed immunization windows.

Potential real-world application
--------------------------------
District health teams can integrate this with existing reporting portals and maintenance workflows. The system can prioritize at-risk clinics, accelerate response decisions, and generate clear escalation paths when risk becomes critical.

Adoption plan
-------------
I plan to start with a pilot in one district, collect operational feedback, and tune thresholds to local conditions. Then I will scale through phased onboarding, training guides, and impact dashboards that show dose-loss reduction and response-time gains.

Closing
-------
I built this as a practical agentic system for public-good operations, and I believe this pattern can be replicated in other essential service networks where delayed decisions create outsized community harm.
