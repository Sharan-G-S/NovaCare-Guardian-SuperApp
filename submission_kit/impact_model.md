Impact Model
============

Objective
---------
I measure whether the system improves incident response quality and protects critical biologic inventory.

Primary Metrics
---------------
- Mean time from incident detection to escalation decision.
- Percentage of incidents resolved before dose compromise.
- Avoidable spoilage volume over rolling 30 day windows.
- Rate of policy-compliant response execution.

Secondary Metrics
-----------------
- False positive rate of high or critical alerts.
- Missed critical incident rate.
- Operator override frequency and reasons.
- Cross-district adoption velocity.

Baseline and Target Example
---------------------------
- Escalation decision time: reduce from 95 minutes to 35 minutes.
- Avoidable spoilage: reduce from 8.5 percent to below 3 percent.
- Policy-compliant response execution: increase from 62 percent to above 90 percent.

Data Collection Plan
--------------------
- Capture all incident traces and action outcomes.
- Compare pre-deployment and pilot-period district metrics.
- Run weekly review loops with field operators.

Risk Notes
----------
- Incomplete sensor coverage can bias severity outcomes.
- Regional policy variance requires local threshold calibration.
- Operational adoption requires training and governance controls.
