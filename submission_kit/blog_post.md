Blog Post Draft
===============

Working Title
-------------
How I built a multimodal agentic system to protect life-saving biologics in climate-disrupted regions

Introduction
------------
I built this project to address a real operational gap in health logistics. In flood-prone districts, biologic cold-chain reliability can collapse quickly, and teams are forced to make urgent decisions from fragmented evidence.

What I Built
------------
I created Nova Cold Chain Guardian, a multimodal multi-agent system that combines temperature telemetry, visual inspection evidence, and field voice transcripts. It does not stop at summarization. It computes severity, explains root causes, and proposes concrete actions.

Why This Matters for Communities
--------------------------------
When antivenom or rabies post-exposure doses are compromised, treatment windows shrink and outcomes worsen. Faster, more accurate triage helps protect scarce inventory and improves continuity of care where margin for error is low.

How It Works in Practice
------------------------
I run each incident through specialist agents and a policy scorer. This structure gives me both intelligent interpretation and deterministic guardrails. Teams get explainable outputs they can trust during high-pressure operations.

Real-World Deployment Path
--------------------------
I plan to pilot in one district, measure response time and spoilage reduction, and iterate on policy thresholds with local operators. Once stable, I can scale to additional districts with training playbooks and operations dashboards.

Adoption Enablement
-------------------
I am focusing on low-friction integration so existing health teams can adopt incrementally. I include traceability, human override, and practical escalation workflows to build trust from the start.

Closing
-------
I built this as a real decision system for real constraints. My goal is simple: reduce preventable loss of life-saving biologics by helping teams act earlier and with higher confidence.
