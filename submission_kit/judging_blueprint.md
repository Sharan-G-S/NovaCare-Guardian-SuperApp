Judging Blueprint
=================

Technical Implementation 60 percent
-----------------------------------
What I must prove
- My architecture is modular, reliable, and production-oriented.
- Amazon Nova capabilities are integrated meaningfully, not superficially.
- The system handles difficult edge cases without silent failure.

Evidence I will present
- End-to-end incident run with agent traces and policy rationale.
- Multimodal evidence fusion from sensor readings, voice transcript, and visual notes.
- Deterministic policy scoring alongside model-driven reasoning.
- Test suite coverage for critical severity boundaries and missing evidence paths.

Demo checkpoints
- Ingest incident payload.
- Show agent-by-agent outputs.
- Show policy severity and risk score logic.
- Show recommended actions and automation plan.

Enterprise or Community Impact 20 percent
-----------------------------------------
What I must prove
- The solution improves operations in a measurable way.
- The deployment path is realistic for constrained environments.

Evidence I will present
- Baseline and target metrics for response time and dose loss.
- Rollout model from one district pilot to multi-district operations.
- Risk governance approach including human override and trace auditing.

Creativity and Innovation 20 percent
------------------------------------
What I must prove
- The approach is novel compared to common hackathon copilots.
- Multi-agent collaboration solves a real operational bottleneck.

Evidence I will present
- Specialist agents with different reasoning scopes and confidence factors.
- Conflict-aware risk calculation when evidence is sparse or contradictory.
- Actionable automation outputs for downstream systems.

Scoring Defense Statements
--------------------------
- I built this as an operational decision system, not a chatbot wrapper.
- I designed for ambiguity and failure modes, not only happy-path inputs.
- I focused on a high-impact domain where faster decisions protect lives.
